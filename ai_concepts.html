Artificial Intelligence (AI)

What it is: The broadest concept. Any technique that enables computers to mimic human intelligence, using logic, if-then rules, decision trees, or machine learning.

Real-World Examples:
  • Non-player characters (NPCs) in video games
  • Rule-based chess engines (like Stockfish)
  • Roomba robot vacuums navigating obstacles
📈

Machine Learning (ML)

What it is: A subset of AI that includes mathematical algorithms allowing machines to improve at tasks with experience. The machine learns from data instead of being explicitly programmed.

Real-World Examples:
  • Email spam filters
  • Netflix or Spotify recommendation algorithms
  • Zillow housing price predictions
🧠

Deep Learning (DL)

What it is: A subset of ML composed of algorithms that permit software to train itself to perform tasks by exposing multi-layered (deep) neural networks to vast amounts of data.

Real-World Examples:
  • Tesla Autopilot (Computer Vision)
  • Medical imaging tumor detection
  • Speech-to-text transcription (e.g., Siri)
✨

Generative AI

What it is: A subset of DL (mostly) that can generate new, novel content—like text, images, audio, or code—rather than simply categorizing or predicting existing data.

Real-World Examples:
  • Midjourney / DALL-E (Image generation)
  • Sora (Video generation)
  • Suno / Udio (Music generation)
💬

Large Language Models (LLMs)

What it is: A specialized subset of Generative AI focused exclusively on understanding and generating human language, trained on massive datasets using transformer architectures.

Real-World Examples:
  • OpenAI's ChatGPT (GPT-4)
  • Anthropic's Claude
  • Google's Gemini

Bridging the Gap

While inspired by biology, artificial neural networks simplify and abstract many complex biological processes.

⚡

Signal Type

Biological
Discrete binary electrical spikes (Action Potentials) occurring over time.
Artificial
Continuous floating-point values computed statically in layers.
🎓

Learning Mechanism

Biological
Hebbian learning, neuroplasticity, localized chemical changes over time.
Artificial
Global optimization via Backpropagation and Gradient Descent.
🕸️

Topology

Biological
Highly complex, recurrent, asynchronous, 3D spatial connections.
Artificial
Rigid layers, mostly feed-forward, synchronized, abstract graphs.
⏱️

Speed & Efficiency

Biological
Slow signals (~100m/s) but massively parallel. Runs on ~20 Watts.
Artificial
Fast signals (speed of light) but relies on heavy GPU computation. High power use.
💡
Fun Fact: The human brain contains roughly 86 billion neurons and over 100 trillion synapses (connections). State-of-the-art AI models like GPT-4 have over 1 trillion parameters, approaching the raw scale of the brain, though functioning differently.
🔄
Activation Functions: The biological equivalent of an activation function is the Action Potential Threshold. A biological neuron doesn't fire strongly or weakly — it fires completely or not at all, closer to a Step Function than a modern continuous ReLU.
